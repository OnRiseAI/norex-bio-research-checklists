# Retatrutide Research Supplier Checklist

This checklist is intended for research buyers evaluating suppliers of Retatrutide materials for in vitro laboratory use.

Norex Bio supplies materials for in vitro laboratory research only. Nothing in this document is medical, diagnostic, veterinary, or therapeutic guidance.

## 1. Confirm the supplier states the intended use clearly

A serious supplier should make the research-use boundary visible before purchase.

Look for language such as:

- Research Use Only (RUO)
- In vitro laboratory research only
- Not for human use
- Not for medical, diagnostic, veterinary, or therapeutic use

Avoid suppliers that blur this boundary with outcome promises, dosing language, treatment language, or consumer-medical claims.

## 2. Check batch identity

A credible listing should make it easy to identify the current batch or lot.

Useful signals include:

- Current batch or lot number
- Product strength or format
- Manufacturing or release context
- Documentation tied to that batch rather than generic marketing claims

If a supplier cannot identify the batch being sold, the buyer cannot meaningfully verify documentation.

## 3. Review purity and testing method

For Retatrutide research sourcing, purity claims should be connected to a testing method, not just stated as marketing copy.

Useful signals include:

- COA availability
- HPLC or comparable analytical method reference
- Batch-specific purity figure
- Clear document date or batch match

Red flags include vague “high purity” claims, screenshots without batch identifiers, or documents that do not match the listed product.

## 4. Evaluate cold-chain handling

Retatrutide research materials are commonly handled with refrigerated logistics. A supplier should explain how the material is stored and dispatched.

Useful questions:

- Is dispatch refrigerated?
- What temperature range is used?
- Where does the shipment originate?
- Is a cold-chain breach indicator used?
- What happens if the product arrives damaged or out of specification?

## 5. Inspect format and handling requirements

Research buyers should understand what format they are ordering and what preparation steps are required.

Compare:

- Pre-filled research pen format
- Vial or lyophilized material
- Reconstitution requirements
- Concentration and handling clarity
- Storage instructions after arrival

Unclear format descriptions increase the risk of ordering the wrong material for the protocol.

## 6. Watch for supplier red flags

Be careful with suppliers that show several of the following:

- No batch number
- No COA or testing method
- No clear storage or shipping information
- No company contact path
- No refund/replacement policy for damaged or out-of-spec delivery
- Therapeutic claims or before/after claims
- Unrealistic delivery promises
- Pages copied across many unrelated domains

## 7. Use a simple evaluation score

A practical supplier screen can use this structure:

- Batch identity visible: yes/no
- COA or analytical method available: yes/no
- Cold-chain details explained: yes/no
- RUO boundary clear: yes/no
- Contact/support path visible: yes/no
- Replacement policy explained: yes/no

If several items are missing, treat the supplier as higher risk.
